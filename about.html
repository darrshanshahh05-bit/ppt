Our Story

What Does "PPT" Mean to Us?

To the world, PPT might stand for PowerPoint Presentation... But for us, it holds a far deeper, more profound meaning:

P Prabhu — The Divine
P Premi — One who loves with devotion
T Trust — A solemn responsibility to serve

Prabhu Premi Trust is not just a name — it's our identity, our inspiration, and our pledge. It symbolizes a life led by faith, compassion, and purposeful action.

Philosophy

Our Philosophy

"Only a true Prabhu Premi — one who loves the Divine with a pure heart — feels the inner call to give, uplift, and serve without expectation."

Spiritual Gateway

At Prabhu Premi Trust (PPT), we are a spiritual gateway for good deeds — a one-stop destination for those who wish to turn gratitude into action.

Nurturing the Spirit

Our goal is not only to serve those in need, but to nurture the spirit of a Prabhu Premi within them too. Because true giving doesn't just change lives — it awakens souls.

Divine Share

We believe that when you are fortunate enough to receive God's grace, the greatest act of devotion is to share it, creating a cycle of kindness and dignity.

"Give with love. Serve with purpose. Grow with faith."

Jain Principles

Guiding Principles

Our mission is rooted in the eternal wisdom of Jain philosophy, embodying ancient truths for a modern path of service.

Ahimsa

Non-violence in thought, word, and deed — the foundation of all our actions and the highest form of spiritual practice.

Aparigraha

Non-possessiveness and non-attachment — giving freely without clinging to material possessions or expectations.

Anekantavada

Many-sided truth — respecting multiple perspectives and embracing the complexity of existence with humility.

Satya

Truthfulness — committing to honesty and transparency in every interaction and transaction.

Brahmacharya

Self-discipline — channeling our energy toward spiritual growth and selfless service to others.

Spiritual Leadership

Women-Led & Jainism Inspired

In the path of Tirthankara Mahavira, leadership is not about power, but about the purity of the soul (Atma-Shakti). Our foundation is deeply rooted in the Chaturvidha Sangha — the four-fold order established to preserve and propagate the eternal truths of Jainism.

Women have always held a position of profound spiritual authority in Jainism. From the extraordinary numbers of Sadhvis in the Sangha of Tirthankara Rishabhdev to the fearless leadership of women today, we embody Shakti as the bridge between devotion and action. Our guiding light is the principle of Anukampa (universal compassion), which dictates that true service must be rendered with equanimity toward all living beings.
